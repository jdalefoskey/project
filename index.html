SUP in the Triangle

This page is for anyone who is looking for places in triangle area of North Carolina to go paddle boarding. My name is Dale, and I live in Chapel Hill, North Carolina and I love to paddle board. I started paddling about 4 years ago, and after my first trip, I knew I had to have paddle board of my own. I did a lot of research and ultimately decided to purchase an inflableble paddle board as my first board. My choice was based on the portability, and after reading through lots of customer reviews I dediced on a Blackfin XL, made by Irocker paddle board company. Check it out and purchase it here. After awhile I decided to upgrade to a non inflatable, and after some research, I purchased from a local company in Cary, NC called Earth Creature and still use that board today.

Primitive spots - places to go for free

For those of you who are adventure seekers and like to detour off the beaten path, these are some cool spots to go. They are mostly primitive spots and will not have modern convienances. So if you visit these spots be ready for some off road parking and walking through the brush sometimes, but you wont regret it.

Family and pet friendly spots

If your whole family loves to paddle or if you like to have paved parking and bathrooms near by. Then you would want to visit these spots. They all have paved parking lots, public bathrooms, and most have beach access and picnic tables. So bring the family, dog, and the hotdogs for the grill and stay all day. Some of these places listed here may require a small fee per car to enter for the day, but thats the price to pay to not have to pee in the bush.

  • Seaforth Beach at Jordan Lake Seaforth Beach Rd Pittsboro, NC 27312
  • Beaver Dam Beach at Falls Lake 14600 Creedmoor Rd Wake Forest NC 27587
  • Falls Lake State Recreation Area 13304 Creedmoor Rd Wake Forest, NC 27587
  • Ferrington Boat Ramp at 605 Farrington Point Rd Chapel Hill, NC 27517
